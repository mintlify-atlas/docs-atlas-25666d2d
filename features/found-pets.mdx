Found a lost pet? The Found Pets feature helps you quickly connect with owners by matching your report against active lost pet searches in Mar del Plata. Your kindness could reunite a family with their beloved companion.

## How to Report a Found Pet

<Steps>
  <Step title="Take Clear Photos">
    Before creating a report, photograph the pet:
    - Front view showing face and markings
    - Side profile showing body shape and size
    - Close-ups of any distinctive features (collar, tags, scars)
    - Any visible identification (tags, microchip scanner results)
    
    If the pet is moving or nervous, prioritize safety and get what photos you can.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Note the Location">
    Record exactly where you found the pet:
    - Use the map to drop a pin at the precise location
    - Add landmarks or cross-streets as reference
    - Note if the pet was moving in a particular direction
    - Include details about the area (park, street, residential area)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create Your Report">
    Fill in the found pet form with:
    - Pet species and estimated breed
    - Physical description (color, size, approximate age)
    - Condition (healthy, injured, wearing collar, etc.)
    - Your contact information
    - Where you've taken the pet (your home, veterinary clinic, shelter)
    - Your availability for owner pickup
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review Potential Matches">
    The system immediately:
    - Scans active lost pet reports in the area
    - Sends notifications to potential matches
    - Shows you similar lost pet reports for manual review
    - Connects you with owners who reach out
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to Do with the Pet

### Immediate Actions

<Tip>
  **Check for Identification**: Look for collar tags, QR code tags, or tattoos. Take the pet to a nearby veterinary clinic to scan for a microchip (free service).
</Tip>

### Temporary Care Options

**Option 1: Keep the Pet Temporarily**
- Provide food, water, and a safe space
- Separate from your own pets initially
- Note any medical needs or injuries
- Update your report with your address for pickup

**Option 2: Take to a Veterinary Clinic**
- Many clinics accept found pets temporarily
- They can scan for microchips
- Provide medical care if needed
- List the clinic location in your report

**Option 3: Contact Local Animal Shelters**
- Mar del Plata has partnered shelters that accept found pets
- They hold pets while searching for owners
- Note the shelter name and pet ID number in your report

<Warning>
  **Safety First**: If the pet appears aggressive or injured, contact local animal control or a veterinary clinic for professional assistance. Your safety is paramount.
</Warning>

## Verifying Ownership

When someone claims the pet is theirs:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request Proof of Ownership">
    Ask for:
    - Additional photos with the owner (dated before the pet was lost)
    - Veterinary records showing the pet's name
    - Microchip registration documents
    - Specific details only the owner would know (favorite toy, unique behaviors)
    - License or adoption papers
  </Step>

  <Step title="Communicate Through the Platform">
    Initial conversations should happen through Mascotas messaging:
    - Share details about where and when you found the pet
    - Ask specific questions about the pet's appearance
    - Verify the description matches what you observe
    - Look for emotional connection and genuine concern
  </Step>

  <Step title="Arrange a Safe Meeting">
    When you're confident about ownership:
    - Meet in a public, well-lit location
    - Bring a friend if possible
    - Observe the pet's reaction to the claimed owner
    - Verify ID matches the platform profile
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete the Reunion">
    Once ownership is confirmed:
    - Mark your report as "Reunited"
    - Share the happy outcome (optional)
    - Accept any offered reward graciously
    - Update the timeline with reunion details
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  **Trust Your Instincts**: If something feels wrong about a claim, request additional verification. Legitimate owners will understand and appreciate your diligence.
</Note>

## Report Features

### Information You Can Include

**Essential Details:**
- Species and breed (or best guess)
- Primary and secondary colors
- Size estimate (small, medium, large)
- Sex if identifiable
- Found location and time
- Current location of the pet

**Additional Helpful Details:**
- Collar description and any visible tags
- Temperament (friendly, scared, aggressive)
- Condition (well-groomed, dirty, injured)
- Approximate age
- Any unique markings or features
- Direction the pet was traveling

### Updating Your Report

Keep your report current:

- Add new photos if the pet becomes more comfortable
- Update location if you move the pet
- Note any medical care provided
- Add behavioral observations
- Track people who have inquired
- Mark as resolved when reunited

## Multiple Claims

If several people claim the same found pet:

<Accordion title="How do I handle multiple ownership claims?">
  When multiple people claim the pet, use a systematic approach:
  
  1. Request proof from all claimants simultaneously
  2. Compare the details each person provides about the pet
  3. Ask for veterinary records or microchip registration
  4. Look for specific details only the true owner would know
  5. If possible, arrange for all claimants to meet the pet separately and observe reactions
  6. Trust the pet's behavior - they often recognize their true owner
  7. If you cannot determine ownership, consider involving local animal control
  
  Never feel pressured to release the pet until you're confident about ownership.
</Accordion>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="Am I legally required to report a found pet?">
  In Argentina, if you find a lost pet, you're expected to make reasonable efforts to locate the owner. Reporting through Mascotas fulfills this obligation. Keeping a found pet without attempting to find the owner could be considered theft. Our platform provides documentation that you've made good faith efforts.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I adopt the pet if no owner comes forward?">
  If no owner is found within 30 days and the pet has been properly reported, you may be eligible to adopt them. However, you should:
  1. Continue your report active for at least 30 days
  2. Contact local animal shelters to file a found report there too
  3. Check with a veterinarian about microchip registration
  4. Follow local regulations about waiting periods
  
  After the waiting period, you can transition the pet to the adoption section of Mascotas if you cannot keep them.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if the pet is injured?">
  If the found pet is injured:
  1. Contact a veterinary clinic immediately - many provide emergency care for found animals
  2. Take photos documenting the injuries (for the report and any legal protection)
  3. Note the injuries in your found pet report
  4. Keep receipts for any medical expenses - owners are typically responsible for reimbursement
  5. Mark your report as "Injured - Needs Owner Contact Urgently"
  
  Some veterinarians and animal welfare organizations may cover emergency costs for found animals.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Should I ask for a reward?">
  Never ask for a reward or make returning the pet conditional on payment - this could be considered extortion. However, if the owner offers a reward, you can accept it graciously. If you incurred expenses (veterinary care, food, supplies), it's appropriate to share receipts and ask if the owner can reimburse you, but be prepared for them to decline.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long should I keep my report active?">
  Keep your found pet report active until the pet is reunited with their owner or permanently rehomed. If no owner comes forward after 30 days, update your report to indicate you're seeking adoption options. Don't close the report too soon - owners sometimes take weeks to find the right resources to search.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if I can't keep the pet temporarily?">
  If you cannot provide temporary care:
  1. Create the found pet report immediately with location details
  2. List "Pet still at found location" in your report
  3. Contact these resources:
     - Local animal shelters in Mar del Plata
     - Veterinary clinics that accept found animals
     - Animal rescue organizations
  4. Update your report with where the pet was taken
  5. Stay involved to help verify ownership when someone claims them
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I remain anonymous when reporting?">
  While you need to provide contact information for owners to reach you, you can:
  - Use only your first name publicly
  - Use platform messaging instead of sharing your phone number initially
  - Omit your exact address (just note the pet is "in safe location")
  - Request that the system hide your profile details
  
  However, to successfully reunite the pet, you'll eventually need to coordinate pickup, which requires some contact information.
</Accordion>
