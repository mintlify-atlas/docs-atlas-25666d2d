The Pet Adoption feature connects people looking to adopt with pets in need of homes throughout Mar del Plata. Whether you're a shelter, rescue organization, or individual rehoming a pet, Mascotas makes the adoption process transparent and efficient.

## For Adopters: Finding Your New Companion

<Steps>
  <Step title="Browse Available Pets">
    Explore pets ready for adoption:
    - Filter by species, breed, age, and size
    - Search within specific areas of Mar del Plata
    - View detailed profiles with photos and descriptions
    - Save favorites to compare later
    - Set up alerts for pets matching your criteria
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review Pet Profiles">
    Each adoption listing includes:
    - Multiple photos showing personality
    - Age, breed, size, and health status
    - Temperament and behavioral traits
    - History and background story
    - Special needs or requirements
    - Adoption fee (if any)
    - Vaccination and medical records
  </Step>

  <Step title="Express Interest">
    When you find a potential match:
    - Click "I'm Interested" on the pet's profile
    - Fill out a brief adopter questionnaire
    - Share your living situation and experience
    - Describe what you're looking for in a pet
    - Provide references if required
    - Submit your application through the platform
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect with the Listing Owner">
    After submitting interest:
    - The shelter or current caretaker reviews your application
    - You'll receive a response within 48 hours
    - Schedule a meet-and-greet via platform messaging
    - Ask questions about the pet's needs and behavior
    - Discuss home visit requirements (if applicable)
    - Coordinate next steps
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete the Adoption">
    Once approved:
    - Sign adoption agreement (digital or in-person)
    - Pay adoption fee if applicable
    - Receive all medical records and documentation
    - Get ongoing support and advice from the listing owner
    - Update your profile to show your new family member
  </Step>
</Steps>

## For Listing Owners: Rehoming a Pet

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create an Adoption Listing">
    Click "List Pet for Adoption" and provide:
    - Pet's name, age, and breed
    - Current health status and medical history
    - Vaccination records and spay/neuter status
    - Personality traits and behavioral notes
    - Ideal home environment
    - Why the pet needs a new home
    - Upload 5-10 high-quality photos
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Adoption Requirements">
    Define what you're looking for in an adopter:
    - Home type (house, apartment, with yard)
    - Experience level (first-time owner OK, experienced preferred)
    - Household composition (children, other pets)
    - Activity level and time commitment
    - Financial capability for pet care
    - Willingness to provide updates
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review Applications">
    Manage incoming adoption requests:
    - View adopter profiles and questionnaires
    - Check their adoption history if applicable
    - Request references or additional information
    - Schedule video calls or in-person meetings
    - Conduct home visits (recommended)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select the Best Match">
    Choose an adopter based on:
    - Compatibility with pet's needs
    - Living situation and lifestyle
    - Experience and references
    - Connection during meet-and-greet
    - Commitment level and preparedness
  </Step>

  <Step title="Finalize the Adoption">
    Complete the process:
    - Sign adoption contract through the platform
    - Transfer medical records and documentation
    - Collect adoption fee if charging one
    - Provide transition tips and pet's belongings
    - Arrange follow-up check-ins
    - Mark listing as "Adopted"
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Creating an Effective Listing

<Tip>
  **Photos Are Everything**: Listings with 7+ high-quality photos receive 4x more applications. Show your pet's personality - playing, relaxing, interacting with people. Avoid dark or blurry photos.
</Tip>

### Essential Information

**Basic Details:**
- Name, age, sex, breed
- Size and weight
- Color and markings
- Spayed/neutered status
- Microchip information
- Current location in Mar del Plata

**Health Information:**
- Vaccination status (upload certificates)
- Last vet visit date
- Any medical conditions or medications
- Special dietary needs
- Dental health
- Energy level

**Behavioral Profile:**
- Temperament (friendly, shy, energetic, calm)
- Socialization level
- Good with children? (specify ages)
- Good with dogs? Cats? Other animals?
- House trained? Crate trained?
- Known commands or training
- Any behavioral challenges (be honest!)

**Ideal Home:**
- Indoor/outdoor preferences
- Space requirements
- Activity level needs
- Alone time tolerance
- Experience level needed

<Note>
  **Honesty Builds Trust**: Be transparent about any behavioral issues or special needs. The right adopter will appreciate honesty and be better prepared to provide appropriate care.
</Note>

## Adoption Fees

### Setting a Fee

Adoption fees are optional but recommended:

- **Typical Range**: $1,000 - $5,000 ARS depending on pet type, age, and included services
- **Purpose**: Ensures adopter is financially prepared, covers medical costs
- **What's Included**: Specify vaccinations, spay/neuter, microchip, supplies
- **Flexibility**: Note if fee is negotiable for the right home

<Info>
  **Fee vs. Free**: Pets with reasonable adoption fees often find better homes. The fee demonstrates the adopter's commitment and financial capability to provide care.
</Info>

### What Fees Typically Cover

- Spay/neuter surgery
- Current vaccinations
- Microchip registration
- Deworming and flea treatment
- Initial vet check-up
- Supplies (food, toys, bed, carrier)

## Application Review Process

### Evaluating Adopters

When reviewing applications, consider:

<Accordion title="Living Situation">
  - Do they own or rent? (If renting, have they confirmed pet policy?)
  - Type of home and yard situation
  - Neighborhood safety and pet-friendliness
  - Space appropriate for pet's size
  - Secure fencing if required
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Experience Level">
  - Previous pet ownership history
  - Understanding of breed-specific needs
  - Training knowledge and willingness to learn
  - Realistic expectations
  - References from previous veterinarians
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Household Composition">
  - All family members agree on adoption
  - Ages of children and pet experience
  - Other pets and their temperaments
  - Activity level and daily routine
  - Who will be primary caretaker
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Financial Readiness">
  - Understanding of ongoing costs (food, vet, grooming)
  - Emergency fund for medical issues
  - Pet insurance consideration
  - Stable income and housing situation
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Time Commitment">
  - Daily schedule and time at home
  - Plan for exercise and mental stimulation
  - Arrangements during work hours
  - Vacation and travel plans
  - Long-term commitment understanding (10-20 years)
</Accordion>

## Meet-and-Greet Best Practices

### For Listing Owners

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a Neutral Location">
    Meet in a public park or neutral space where the pet feels comfortable. This helps you observe the pet's behavior without home territory concerns.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Prepare Your Pet">
    Ensure the pet is well-exercised and calm before the meeting. Bring favorite toys or treats. Have medical records and care instructions ready.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Observe Interactions">
    Watch how the potential adopter interacts with your pet. Do they listen to your guidance? Is the pet comfortable? Are children gentle?
  </Step>

  <Step title="Answer Questions Honestly">
    Be prepared to discuss challenges, quirks, and needs. Share daily routine, favorite activities, and any behavioral tips.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### For Potential Adopters

<Steps>
  <Step title="Bring the Whole Family">
    Everyone who lives in the home should meet the pet. This helps ensure compatibility and commitment from all household members.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ask Detailed Questions">
    Inquire about daily routine, favorite foods, behavioral quirks, medical history, and what situations cause stress or anxiety.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Meet Multiple Times If Possible">
    Request a second visit to see the pet in different contexts. Consider bringing your current pets for introduction (if applicable).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Trust Your Instincts">
    It's okay if it's not the right match. The goal is finding the best fit for both you and the pet.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  **Red Flags**: Be cautious of adopters who: refuse home visits, seem unprepared or impulsive, can't answer basic pet care questions, have history of rehoming multiple pets, or pressure you to decide quickly.
</Warning>

The platform provides customizable adoption contract templates:

### Standard Clauses Include:

- Adopter's commitment to provide proper care
- Agreement to maintain medical care and vaccinations
- Return policy if adoption doesn't work out
- Spay/neuter requirements (if not already done)
- Contact information updates
- Right to request welfare check

## Post-Adoption Support

### Stay Connected

Many listing owners appreciate:
- 1-week check-in to see how pet is adjusting
- 30-day update with photos
- Ongoing availability for questions
- Updates on pet's life in their new home

### Return Policies

Consider including in your adoption agreement:

- Trial period (common: 2 weeks) where pet can be returned
- Requirement to return pet to you (not surrender to shelter)
- Partial fee refund policy
- Conditions under which return is acceptable

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="How long does the adoption process typically take?">
  The timeline varies but typically:
  - Application review: 2-5 days
  - Initial conversation: 1-3 days
  - Meet-and-greet scheduling: 3-7 days
  - Home visit and decision: 1-3 days
  - Paperwork and pickup: 1-2 days
  
  Total: 1-3 weeks on average. Some adoptions happen faster if there's an immediate connection, while others take longer to ensure the right fit.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can shelters and rescues use Mascotas for adoptions?">
  Absolutely! Shelters and rescue organizations are encouraged to use Mascotas. You can:
  - Create an organization account
  - List multiple pets simultaneously
  - Manage all applications from one dashboard
  - Use custom adoption requirements and contracts
  - Track adoption statistics and success rates
  - Promote your organization's other services
  
  Contact us for shelter/rescue account benefits and features.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if I receive no applications for my pet?">
  If your listing isn't getting interest:
  1. Review and improve your photos - they're the most important element
  2. Lower adoption fee or make it negotiable
  3. Add more details about personality and daily life
  4. Share your listing on social media and local groups
  5. Consider expanding your search radius
  6. Be honest about any challenges but emphasize positives
  7. Update the listing weekly to keep it visible
  8. Consider lowering adoption requirements if they're very strict
  
  Our support team can review your listing and provide specific recommendations.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Should I do a home visit before finalizing adoption?">
  Home visits are highly recommended, especially for dogs and pets with special needs. They allow you to:
  - Verify the living situation matches what was described
  - Check for safety hazards (unfenced pool, toxic plants, etc.)
  - Ensure adequate space and pet-proofing
  - Meet all family members in their home environment
  - Provide specific advice for their setup
  - Feel confident about your decision
  
  Most adopters understand and expect home visits. If someone refuses, consider it a red flag.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What's the difference between adoption and rehoming?">
  On Mascotas, we use "adoption" for both:
  - **Shelter/Rescue Adoption**: Organizations rehoming stray, abandoned, or surrendered pets
  - **Private Rehoming**: Individual owners finding new homes for their personal pets
  
  Both use the same platform features. Private rehoming is often faster and provides more direct history about the pet's background and personality.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I adopt a pet from outside Mar del Plata?">
  Yes, you can view and apply for pets listed in nearby areas. However:
  - You'll need to arrange transportation
  - Meet-and-greets may require travel
  - Home visits might be challenging for the listing owner
  - Consider local options first to simplify the process
  
  Some listing owners are willing to transport pets for the right home.
</Accordion>
