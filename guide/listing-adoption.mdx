## List a Pet for Adoption

Whether you're rehoming your own pet, fostering, or working with a rescue organization, Mascotas helps you find loving homes for pets in Mar del Plata.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access the Adoption Form">
    From your dashboard, click **List Pet for Adoption**. You can also navigate to **Adoption** > **Create Listing** from the menu.

    <Info>
      You must have a verified account to list pets for adoption. This helps ensure accountability and pet safety.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload Quality Photos">
    Upload 5-10 high-quality photos showing:
    - Clear face shots from different angles
    - Full body photos showing size and build
    - Photos interacting with people or other pets
    - Any unique markings or features
    - The pet in different settings (playing, resting, etc.)

    <Tip>
      Great photos significantly increase adoption interest. Use natural lighting, show the pet's personality, and avoid blurry or dark images.
    </Tip>

    <Warning>
      Only upload photos of pets you have legal authority to rehome. Listing someone else's pet without permission is prohibited.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Provide Complete Pet Information">
    Fill out detailed information about the pet:
    
    **Basic Details**
    - Pet name
    - Species (dog, cat, or other)
    - Breed (or mix/unknown)
    - Age (or estimated age)
    - Gender
    - Size (small, medium, large)
    - Weight
    - Colors and markings

    **Health Information**
    - Vaccination status (up to date, needs updates, unknown)
    - Spayed/neutered status
    - Microchipped (yes/no and chip number if yes)
    - Current health conditions or special needs
    - Recent veterinary care
    - Medications (if any)

    <Note>
      Complete health information builds trust with potential adopters and helps them prepare for the pet's needs.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Describe Personality and Behavior">
    Write a detailed description of the pet's temperament:
    - Energy level (low, moderate, high)
    - Friendliness with people (all ages, children, strangers)
    - Behavior with other pets (dogs, cats, small animals)
    - Training level (housebroken, basic commands, special training)
    - Activity preferences (walks, play, cuddles, independent)
    - Any behavioral quirks or challenges

    <Tip>
      Be honest about behavioral challenges. Setting realistic expectations leads to successful, lasting adoptions.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Adoption Requirements">
    Specify what you're looking for in an adopter:
    
    **Home Requirements**
    - Living situation (house with yard, apartment, etc.)
    - Landlord approval required (if renting)
    - Secure fencing needed (for dogs)
    - No small children (if applicable)
    
    **Adopter Qualifications**
    - Experience with pets (required/preferred)
    - Time availability for pet care
    - Financial stability for vet care
    - Reference check required
    
    **Adoption Process**
    - Application required (yes/no)
    - Home visit required (yes/no)
    - Meet and greet mandatory
    - Trial period offered

    <Info>
      Thorough screening helps ensure the pet goes to a safe, appropriate home. Most adopters appreciate the careful process.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Adoption Fee (Optional)">
    Decide if you'll charge an adoption fee:
    - **No Fee**: Free adoption to good home
    - **Cost Recovery**: Fee covers vaccination, spay/neuter costs
    - **Standard Fee**: Typical adoption fee (usually $2,000-$8,000 ARS)

    <Note>
      Adoption fees help ensure serious adopters and can offset veterinary costs. However, some rescues offer free adoptions during special events.
    </Note>

    Include what the fee covers:
    - Vaccinations
    - Spay/neuter surgery
    - Microchip
    - Initial health check
    - Supplies (collar, leash, food, etc.)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Your Story (Optional but Recommended)">
    Share the pet's background:
    - How you came to have the pet
    - Why they need a new home
    - What makes them special
    - What kind of home would be ideal
    - Your hopes for their future

    <Tip>
      Personal stories create emotional connections. They help adopters understand the pet's journey and envision them in their home.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Location and Contact Preferences">
    Provide meeting and contact information:
    - **Pet's Current Location**: Neighborhood in Mar del Plata
    - **Meeting Location Preference**: Your home, neutral location, etc.
    - **Available Times**: When you can meet with interested adopters
    - **Contact Method**: WhatsApp, email, or phone
    - **Response Time**: How quickly you typically respond

    <Warning>
      For safety, consider meeting interested adopters in public locations first before arranging home visits.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and Publish">
    Before publishing, review:
    - All photos are clear and appropriate
    - Information is accurate and complete
    - Contact details are correct
    - Adoption requirements are reasonable
    
    When ready:
    1. Check the box confirming accuracy
    2. Agree to platform adoption guidelines
    3. Click **Publish Listing**

    Your listing will be reviewed (usually within 24 hours) and then published to the adoption section.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Best Practices for Successful Adoptions

<Accordion title="Writing an effective pet description">
  Your description is crucial for attracting the right adopter:
  
  **Do:**
  - Start with the pet's most lovable qualities
  - Use specific examples of their personality
  - Be honest about challenges
  - Describe their ideal home environment
  - Use warm, engaging language
  - Highlight what makes them unique
  
  **Don't:**
  - Use only generic descriptions
  - Hide behavioral or health issues
  - Write extremely long descriptions (keep it 2-3 paragraphs)
  - Use all caps or excessive punctuation
  - Make unrealistic claims
  
  <Tip>
    Example: Instead of "Nice dog," write: "Luna is a gentle soul who loves morning walks and afternoon naps. She's great with kids over 8 and would thrive in a calm home where she can be your loyal companion."
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Screening potential adopters">
  Ask important questions to find the right match:
  
  **Initial Questions**
  - Why are you looking to adopt?
  - Have you had pets before?
  - Who lives in your household?
  - What is your living situation?
  - What are your work hours?
  
  **Pet-Specific Questions**
  - Are you prepared for [specific needs]?
  - How will you handle [behavioral challenges]?
  - What's your budget for pet care?
  - Who will care for the pet when you travel?
  
  **Red Flags**
  - Unwillingness to answer questions
  - Plans to keep pet outside only
  - No budget for veterinary care
  - Wants pet as a gift for someone else
  - Rushing the process
  
  <Info>
    Trust your instincts. If something feels off, it's okay to decline an adoption.
  </Info>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Conducting meet-and-greets">
  Meeting in person is essential:
  
  **First Meeting**
  - Meet in a neutral, safe location (park, pet store, etc.)
  - Bring the pet's medical records
  - Observe how the adopter interacts with the pet
  - Watch the pet's reaction to the person
  - Discuss expectations and responsibilities
  
  **What to Look For**
  - Gentle, patient handling
  - Asking thoughtful questions
  - Showing genuine interest in the pet's needs
  - Willingness to follow your recommendations
  
  **Second Meeting (if applicable)**
  - Home visit to see the environment
  - Meet all household members
  - Check if the home is pet-proofed
  - Ensure fencing is secure (for dogs)
  
  <Tip>
    Consider a trial adoption period of 1-2 weeks to ensure it's a good fit for both the pet and adopter.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Finalizing the adoption">
  When you've found the right adopter:
  
  **Documentation**
  - Complete an adoption contract (template available on Mascotas)
  - Transfer microchip registration to new owner
  - Provide all medical records
  - Share pet's routine and preferences
  
  **Transition Supplies**
  - Give some of their current food (for gradual transition)
  - Include favorite toys or blanket
  - Provide collar, leash, and any medications
  - Share contact info for current veterinarian
  
  **Follow-Up**
  - Offer to answer questions after adoption
  - Request updates on how the pet is settling in
  - Be available for a transition period
  
  <Info>
    Some adopters appreciate a "return policy" where they can return the pet within 1-2 weeks if it's truly not working out. This ensures the pet doesn't end up in a shelter.
  </Info>
</Accordion>

## Managing Your Adoption Listing

### Updating Your Listing

To edit your adoption listing:
1. Go to **My Listings** in your dashboard
2. Select the adoption listing
3. Click **Edit Listing**
4. Make your changes
5. Click **Save Changes**

<Note>
  Significant changes require re-review by the platform. Minor updates (like adding photos or clarifying text) are published immediately.
</Note>

### Marking as Adopted

When the pet has been adopted:
1. Go to your listing
2. Click **Mark as Adopted**
3. Optionally share adoption success story and photo
4. Confirm the action

This will:
- Close the listing to new inquiries
- Notify interested users that the pet was adopted
- Add success story to community feed
- Keep listing in your history

<Tip>
  Sharing adoption success stories encourages others to adopt and showcases positive outcomes.
</Tip>

### Pausing Your Listing

If you need to temporarily pause your listing:
1. Go to your listing
2. Click **Pause Listing**
3. Provide a reason (optional)
4. Confirm

Paused listings:
- Don't appear in search results
- Can be reactivated anytime
- Preserve all information and inquiries

Use this feature if:
- You're evaluating a trial adoption
- You need time to catch up on inquiries
- The pet has temporary health issues
