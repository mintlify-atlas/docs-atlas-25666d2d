## Contacting Pet Owners on Mascotas

Mascotas makes it easy to connect pet owners and community members through WhatsApp integration and secure messaging. This guide shows you how to reach out effectively.

## How to Contact an Owner

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the Report or Listing">
    Navigate to the pet report or adoption listing you want to respond to:
    - Browse the [map view](/features/map-search) for nearby pets
    - Search by breed, location, or date
    - Check your dashboard for matched reports

    Click on the pet's card to view the full details.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review All Information">
    Before contacting, thoroughly review:
    - Pet description and photos
    - Location details
    - Owner's notes and requirements
    - Posted date and any updates

    <Tip>
      Understanding all the details helps you provide relevant, helpful information when you reach out.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose Contact Method">
    On the report page, you'll see available contact options:
    
    **WhatsApp (Recommended)**
    - Click the green **Contact via WhatsApp** button
    - Opens WhatsApp with pre-filled message
    - Fastest way to reach owners
    - Allows photo sharing

    **In-Platform Message**
    - Click **Send Message** for privacy-protected messaging
    - Keeps your phone number private initially
    - Message history saved in your dashboard

    **Email** (if provided)
    - Click the email address to open your email client
    - Better for detailed information
    - May have slower response time

    <Info>
      Most pet owners prefer WhatsApp because it's fast and allows instant photo sharing for verification.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Use the Pre-filled Message Template">
    When you click **Contact via WhatsApp**, Mascotas provides a helpful template:

    ```
    Hi! I saw your [lost/found/adoption] post on Mascotas about [Pet Name/Description].
    
    [Your message here]
    
    Link to post: [URL]
    ```

    Customize this message with specific, relevant details (see best practices below).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send Your Message">
    After customizing the message:
    1. Review for clarity and completeness
    2. Attach any relevant photos if applicable
    3. Click Send in WhatsApp

    The owner will receive your message and can respond directly through WhatsApp.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Contact Scenarios and Best Practices

<Accordion title="Contacting about a lost pet you've found">
  When you've found a pet that matches a lost pet report:
  
  **What to Include:**
  - Exact location where you found the pet
  - Time and date you found them
  - Current location (if you have the pet)
  - Description of distinctive features you noticed
  - Photos if possible
  - Your availability to reunite them
  
  **Example Message:**
  ```
  Hi! I saw your lost pet post on Mascotas about Luna, the golden retriever.
  
  I found a dog matching this description today at 3pm near Plaza Colón. 
  She's currently at my home (Güemes neighborhood) and seems healthy. 
  She has the white spot on her chest that you mentioned.
  
  I can meet you today to return her. Please verify by describing 
  any other markings she has that aren't in the photos.
  
  [Include photo]
  ```
  
  <Warning>
    Always verify the person is the true owner before handing over the pet. Ask them to describe features not shown in photos.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Contacting about a found pet that might be yours">
  When you see a found pet report that might be your lost pet:
  
  **What to Include:**
  - Your pet's name and when they went missing
  - Specific identifying features to verify it's your pet
  - Your location and availability to pick them up
  - Recent photos of your pet for comparison
  - Offer to provide verification (vet records, microchip info)
  
  **Example Message:**
  ```
  Hi! I saw your found pet post and I believe this might be my dog, Max.
  
  He's been missing since Monday from the Constitución neighborhood. 
  He's a 3-year-old beagle with a small scar above his left eye and 
  a brown spot on his left paw that isn't visible in every photo.
  
  I have vet records and he's microchipped (chip #: 123456789).
  Can I send you photos to compare? I can pick him up immediately.
  ```
  
  <Tip>
    Provide specific details that prove ownership without revealing everything in your first message. This helps protect against pet theft.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Inquiring about a pet for adoption">
  When interested in adopting a pet:
  
  **What to Include:**
  - Why you're interested in this specific pet
  - Your relevant experience with pets
  - Your living situation (house, apartment, yard, etc.)
  - Household members (adults, children, other pets)
  - Your availability for a meet-and-greet
  - Any questions about the pet's needs
  
  **Example Message:**
  ```
  Hi! I'm interested in adopting Milo from your Mascotas listing.
  
  I live in a house with a large fenced yard in Camet. I have experience 
  with senior dogs and I'm looking for a calm companion. I work from home 
  so I'm available during the day.
  
  I'd love to meet Milo and learn more about his medical needs. 
  Are you available for a meet-and-greet this week?
  ```
  
  <Info>
    A thoughtful, detailed first message shows you're a serious, responsible potential adopter and increases your chances of a response.
  </Info>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Reporting a sighting of someone's lost pet">
  When you've seen a lost pet but haven't been able to catch them:
  
  **What to Include:**
  - Exact location of sighting with landmarks
  - Time and date you saw them
  - Direction they were heading
  - Condition they appeared to be in
  - How confident you are it's their pet
  - Your contact info if you'll be in the area again
  
  **Example Message:**
  ```
  Hi! I saw your lost cat post on Mascotas for Tiger.
  
  I'm pretty sure I saw him this morning around 8am near the corner 
  of Jara and Gascón. He was heading toward Parque San Martín.
  He looked healthy but seemed scared.
  
  I tried to approach but he ran. I walk this route every morning 
  and will keep an eye out. Should I try to leave food in the area?
  ```
  
  <Tip>
    Even unconfirmed sightings are valuable. They help owners narrow their search area and adjust their search strategy.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Offering help or resources">
  When you want to help but don't have direct information about the pet:
  
  **What to Include:**
  - Type of help you can offer
  - Your availability
  - Your location or coverage area
  - Any relevant skills or resources
  
  **Example Message:**
  ```
  Hi! I saw your lost dog post on Mascotas.
  
  I live in the Punta Mogotes area and can help distribute flyers 
  if you'd like. I also volunteer at the local shelter and can 
  check if anyone has brought in a dog matching this description.
  
  Let me know if there's any way I can help with the search.
  ```
  
  <Info>
    Community support makes a huge difference. Offering to help distribute flyers, check shelters, or keep watch shows the power of the Mascotas community.
  </Info>
</Accordion>

## Communication Best Practices

### Be Clear and Specific

<Tip>
  Instead of: "I think I saw your dog."
  
  Write: "I saw a brown and white corgi at Plaza España today at 4pm. He was wearing a red collar and heading toward Av. Colón."
</Tip>

### Include Relevant Photos

If you have photos that could help:
- Take clear, well-lit photos
- Include multiple angles
- Show distinctive features
- Compress large files if needed

### Be Respectful of Time

- Don't message repeatedly without response
- Allow at least 12-24 hours for reply
- Understand owners may be overwhelmed with messages
- Be patient and understanding

### Verify Before Acting

<Warning>
  Never give a found pet to someone without proper verification. Ask for:
  - Photos with the pet from before they were lost
  - Descriptions of features not visible in found pet photos
  - Vet records or microchip information
  - The pet's reaction to their name and the person
</Warning>

### Stay Safe

- Meet in public places initially
- Bring a friend if meeting someone you don't know
- Trust your instincts
- Don't share your home address until you feel comfortable

## Managing Your Messages

### In-Platform Messaging

If you use the platform's internal messaging:

1. Access your messages from the dashboard
2. Click **Messages** icon in the top navigation
3. View conversation history with each owner
4. Set notifications for new messages

<Info>
  In-platform messages are saved and can be referenced later, which is helpful for adoption processes that take time.
</Info>

### WhatsApp Conversations

For WhatsApp conversations:
- Keep the Mascotas post link handy
- Save important messages with WhatsApp star feature
- Use WhatsApp status to track conversation state
- Archive conversations after successful reunification

### Response Expectations

**Typical Response Times:**
- Lost/Found Pet Reports: 1-12 hours (urgent)
- Adoption Inquiries: 12-48 hours
- General Questions: 24-72 hours

<Note>
  If you don't receive a response within these timeframes, the owner may have resolved the situation or is handling many inquiries. A polite follow-up after 48 hours is acceptable.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting Contact Issues

<Accordion title="WhatsApp button doesn't work">
  If the WhatsApp contact button isn't working:
  
  1. Ensure you have WhatsApp installed
  2. Check if your browser allows opening WhatsApp links
  3. Try WhatsApp Web if on desktop
  4. Use in-platform messaging as alternative
  5. Check if the owner has WhatsApp contact enabled
  
  <Note>
    Some users prefer to receive in-platform messages first and will provide their WhatsApp number after initial contact.
  </Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="No response from owner">
  If you don't receive a response:
  
  - Check if the report is still active (not marked as found/adopted)
  - Verify you sent to the correct contact
  - Try an alternative contact method if available
  - Wait 48 hours before following up
  - Consider that they may have resolved the situation
  
  If it's urgent (you have their lost pet), try:
  - Commenting on the public report
  - Contacting through the platform admin
  - Taking the pet to a local shelter temporarily
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Owner's phone number not working">
  If the phone number doesn't work:
  
  - Check for typos in your dialing
  - Verify you included country code (+54 for Argentina)
  - Try removing or adding the "9" before area code
  - Use in-platform messaging instead
  - Report the issue if you suspect fake contact info
  
  <Info>
    Argentinian mobile numbers: +54 9 [area code] [number]
    Example: +54 9 223 123-4567 for Mar del Plata
  </Info>
</Accordion>
